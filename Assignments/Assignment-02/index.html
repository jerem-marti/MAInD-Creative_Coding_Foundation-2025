Pong is one of the earliest arcade video games, originally released by Atari in 1972. Created by Allan Alcorn, it became a cultural phenomenon and helped establish the video game industry. The game simulates table tennis, where players control paddles to hit a ball back and forth across the screen.

Play

Rules

The objective of Pong is to score points by making the ball pass your opponent's paddle. Each player controls a paddle that can move along their side of the screen. The ball bounces off the paddles and the top and bottom edges of the screen. If the ball passes a player's paddle, the opposing player scores a point. The game continues until a player reaches a 15-point score.

Rules cheat sheet

  • Score points by making the ball pass your opponent's paddle.
  • The first player to 15 points wins the game.
  • The ball bounces off paddles and screen edges.
  • The ball gets the inertia of the paddle it hits.
  • The ball accelerates slightly after each paddle hit.
  • Players can control their paddles using keyboard or touch input.
